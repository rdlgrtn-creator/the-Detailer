the detailer | Precious Metals: Futures vs. ETFs

Understanding the difference between Notional Value and out-of-pocket cost is the key to trading silver and gold.

Silver Futures (/SI)

One standard Silver Futures contract represents 5,000 troy ounces. At a spot price of $85.91, the math looks like this:

5,000 t oz x $85.91 = $429,550 Notional Value

The Tick Value (the smallest move) is $25. This means a $1.00 move in silver results in a $5,000 change in your account value.

The SLV Alternative

The Silver ETF (SLV) is more manageable. While one futures contract controls $429,550 of silver, one SLV option contract controls 100 shares:

$75.44 (Share Price) x 100 = $7,544 Notional Value
Gold Futures (/GC)

One gold contract controls 100 troy ounces. With gold at $4,887.07, the notional value is $488,707. Traders often use Margin (about $27,000) to control this, creating massive leverage.

The Gold-Silver Ratio (GSR)

This metric tells us which metal is "cheaper" relatively. You divide the price of gold by the price of silver:

$4,887.07 / $85.91 = 56.8

It currently takes about 57 ounces of silver to buy 1 ounce of gold.
