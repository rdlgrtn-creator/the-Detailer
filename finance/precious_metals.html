the detailer | Precious Metals: Futures vs. ETFs

A deep dive into the notional value and leverage of Silver and Gold markets as of February 2026.

Silver: Futures (/SI) vs. SLV

One standard Silver Futures contract represents 5,000 troy ounces. At a spot price of $85.91, the math is staggering:

5,000 t oz x $85.91 = $429,550 Notional Value

The Tick: The minimum move is $0.005. To find the value of a single "tick," we multiply the move by the contract size:

$0.005 x 5,000 = $25.00 per tick

Because there are 200 ticks in a $1.00 move, silver moving just one dollar changes your account value by $5,000.

The SLV Alternative

For those avoiding the massive leverage of futures, the SLV ETF offers a smaller footprint. One SLV option contract controls 100 shares:

$75.44 (Share Price) x 100 = $7,544 Notional Value
Gold: Futures (/GC) vs. GLD

One gold contract controls 100 troy ounces. With gold at $4,887.07, the notional value is $488,707. Traders often use roughly $27,000 in margin to control this, which is massive leverage.

The Gold-Silver Ratio (GSR)

Traders use this to see which metal is relatively "cheap."

$4,887.07 (Gold) / $85.91 (Silver) = 56.8

It currently takes about 57 ounces of silver to purchase 1 ounce of gold.
