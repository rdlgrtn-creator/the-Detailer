40-Year Monte Carlo Retirement Simulator

Model Overview: This advanced Excel model uses a stochastic (randomized) approach to forecast retirement success. Unlike traditional calculators that assume a flat 7% return, this simulation runs 1,000 parallel lives, each facing different market volatilities, to show you the range of possible outcomes.

1. The "Engine" (How it Works)

The model utilizes a nested functional approach:

  • The Outer Loop (MAP): Triggers the simulation 1,000 times to create a massive data set of potential futures.
  • The Timeline (REDUCE): Iterates through a 40-year sequence, carrying your account balances forward year-by-year and applying compound growth and inflation.
  • The Logic (LET): Assigns names to complex calculations (like market returns and tax rules), making the formula efficient and easy to audit.

2. Core Financial Logic

The simulation is built with "Real World" variables to ensure accuracy:

  • Monte Carlo Market Returns: Every year, the model pulls a random return based on a Normal Distribution (using your specific Mean and Standard Deviation). This captures the "Sequence of Returns" riskâ€”the danger of a market crash happening early in retirement.
  • Dynamic "Guardrail" Spending: The model simulates a smart investor. If your Total Wealth (tw) drops below a safety threshold ($1.1M), the model automatically implements a 20% spending cut to preserve capital.
  • Intelligent Drawdown Order: To maximize tax efficiency, the model drains accounts in a specific priority:
    1. Cash/Savings (No tax hit)
    2. Taxable Brokerage (Capital gains environment)
    3. Tax-Advantaged/Insurance (Applied with a 15% tax/penalty buffer, calculated as fdr).
  • Social Security Integration: The model accounts for the exact year your Social Security benefits begin, reducing the strain on your portfolio (dr) once those checks start arriving.

3. How to Interpret the Results

When you run this simulation, you will get 1,000 different "Ending Balances."

  • Success Rate: The percentage of simulations where the ending balance is greater than $0.
  • The Median (50th Percentile): Your "most likely" future.
  • The Bottom 10%: Your "Stress Test" scenario. If you can survive this, your plan is incredibly robust.

Technical Requirements

  • Excel Version: Requires Microsoft 365 or Excel 2021+ (to support LAMBDA, MAP, and REDUCE).
  • Calculation: For best performance, it is recommended to set Calculation Options to Manual while editing, then press F9 to run the 1,000 simulations.
