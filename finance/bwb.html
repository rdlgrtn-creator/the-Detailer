Silver Analysis: Futures (/SI) vs. ETFs (SLV)

For the granular investor, the choice between physical, paper, and derivative exposure comes down to Carry Costs and Leverage Efficiency.

1. Futures (/SI) - The Purest Derivative

  • Contract Size: 5,000 Troy Ounces.
  • Tick Value: $0.005 = $25.00 per contract.
  • The Edge: Futures provide 23-hour access and massive capital efficiency (leverage), but require managing "Roll Yield" as contracts expire.

2. ETFs (SLV) - The Retail Proxy

  • Mechanism: Backed by silver bullion in London vaults (JPMorgan).
  • Expense Ratio: 0.50% annual management fee.
  • The Drawback: No leverage and higher tax implications (taxed as a "collectible" at 28% in many jurisdictions).
